Puzzle

Puzzle is a very easy game to play.   Start Puzzle, then migrate to a Folder that contains Image files.   You may select any picture you like.   You may use the same picture over or select a new one each time.   If you like, I will select one of the Graphic files at random.   I will give you a few seconds to study the picture before I scramble it.   Then all you have to do is to click on any two puzzle pieces, and press the 'Enter' key.   When you press 'Enter', the last two puzzle pieces that you clicked will trade places.   Keep swapping pieces until the Puzzle is solved.   The first puzzle has only four large pieces and should be quite easy, but they get progressively more difficult.   Like any Puzzle, some are easier and some are more difficult than others.

Note:   I temporarily change your DeskTop WallPaper while the program is running, but then I restore it when you exit Puzzle.
Note:   If you click on a wrong piece by mistake, just keep going and click the two pieces that you do want.
Note:   Right click once anywhere on the Puzzle to open the Popup Menu.
Menu
About   displays the About Box with information about Fifteen and the programmer.
Help   of course, will display this Help information.

Hint   will Hi-lite every piece that is out of order.
Solve   will finish the Puzzle for you.   It swaps 2 pieces at a time until the Puzzle is solved.

New Puzzle   will select another image file and start a New Puzzle for you.
Show Puzzle   will show you what the Puzzle is supposed to look like.
Show File Name   will display the name of the picture in the Upper-Left-Hand corner of the screen.   Click it again to turn off the display.

Quit   will end the program.

Tip:   I have added a new function to Puzzle that gives the player an opportunity to specify the number of pieces in the Puzzle.   That function is only available after you complete a Puzzle.   More information will be provided at that time.
